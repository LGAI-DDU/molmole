Abstract

The extraction of molecular structures and reaction data from scientific documents is challenging due to their varied, unstructured chemical formats and complex document layouts. We introduce MolMole, a vision-based deep learning framework that combines molecule detection, reaction diagram parsing, optical chemical structure recognition (OCSR) into a unified pipeline, designed to automate the extraction of chemical data directly from page-level documents. To address the absence of page-level benchmark and evaluation metric, we present a test set of 550 pages annotated with molecule bounding boxes, reaction labels, and MOLfiles, accompanied by a novel evaluation metric. Experimental results demonstrate that MolMole surpasses existing toolkits on our new benchmark and public dataset.
